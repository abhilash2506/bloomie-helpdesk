All FAQ Articles
How many casual leaves am I entitled to? ▼
Employees are entitled to 12 Casual Leaves (CL) per calendar year, credited at 1 per month from the date of joining. CL can be taken in minimum half-day increments and cannot be carried forward.
How do I apply for leave? ▼
Leave must be applied at least 48 hours in advance (except for emergencies) via your HRMS portal or by informing your immediate supervisor. Emergency leaves can be applied on the same day with manager approval.
What is the encashment policy for earned leaves? ▼
Earned Leaves (EL) accumulate at 1.25 days per month (15 per year). You can encash up to 50% of accumulated EL (max 30 days) once per year in October. Minimum 15 days must be maintained in your balance.
How do I correct a wrong attendance punch? ▼
Raise an attendance regularisation request via the helpdesk within 7 days of the incorrect entry. Provide the correct punch time, reason, and manager approval. Corrections beyond 7 days require HR Head approval.
When is salary credited? ▼
Salaries are processed by the 28th of each month and credited to bank accounts by the last working day. In months with holidays, processing may be done 2 days earlier to ensure timely credit.
How do I get my payslip? ▼
Payslips are available in your HRMS portal under Payroll > Payslips. You can download PDF copies for any month. Physical copies can be requested from HR if required for official purposes.
What is the process to update my bank details? ▼
Submit a bank detail update request through the helpdesk with: a cancelled cheque or bank passbook photo, your employee ID, and HR approval. Updates take effect from the next payroll cycle.
How do I file a POSH complaint? ▼
File a written complaint with the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) within 3 months of the incident. Use the confidential POSH category when raising a helpdesk ticket, or email the ICC directly. All complaints are handled with strict confidentiality.
Who are the ICC members? ▼
The ICC comprises: a Presiding Officer (senior woman employee), 2 internal members, 1 external member (NGO/legal background). Details are displayed on the HR notice board at each property and available from HR.
What is the notice period for resignation? ▼
Notice periods are role-dependent: Junior staff (below Executive level) — 30 days; Executive to Manager level — 60 days; Senior Manager and above — 90 days. Notice can be served or bought out with mutual agreement.
How is Full & Final settlement calculated? ▼
F&F includes: pending salary, leave encashment (as per policy), gratuity (if eligible: 5+ years), any reimbursements due. F&F is processed within 45 days of the last working day after clearance from all departments.
What documents are required for joining? ▼
Documents required: Offer letter acceptance, ID proof (Aadhaar/Passport/Driving licence), PAN card, last 3 months' salary slips, relieving letter from previous employer, educational certificates, 2 passport photos, bank details. All originals must be verified.
How long is the probation period? ▼
The standard probation period is 6 months from the date of joining. Confirmation is subject to performance review by the reporting manager and HR. Probation may be extended by up to 3 months if required.
What is the dress code policy? ▼
Guest-facing roles: Uniform as provided must be worn at all times during duty. Back-office roles: Smart casual / business casual is expected. Jeans with formal tops are permitted for non-guest-facing departments on working days.
What is the mobile phone policy during duty? ▼
Personal mobile phones are not permitted in guest areas or kitchen. Back-office staff may use phones for work purposes. Violation may result in disciplinary action. Emergency contact can be made via the department manager.
